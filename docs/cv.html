Profile

Nghia Trung Ngo is a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Oregon. His research focuses on generalized learning settings, including domain adaptation, multilingual learning, multi-task learning, retrieval-augmented generation, and benchmark design for large language models.

Research Experience

Research Assistant, University of Oregon

2022 to present

  • Research topics include transfer learning, multilingual learning, and multi-task learning.
  • Developed work on joint learning for multiple information extraction tasks in domain adaptation settings.
  • Introduced generalized multi-transfer settings for cross-lingual learning.
  • Current directions include medical RAG benchmarking, multilingual knowledge integration, and complex commonsense reasoning benchmarks.

NLP Research Resident, VinAI Research

2020 to 2021

  • Worked on domain adaptation and event extraction.

  • Contributed to an applied project benchmarking vision transformers on fisheye images for autonomous driving.

Research Student, Data Science Laboratory, HUST

2018 to 2019

  • Worked on machine learning foundations and probabilistic topic modeling.
