## 1. Homepage short bio

Nghia Trung Ngo is a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Oregon, advised by Prof. Thien Huu Nguyen. His research focuses on learning in generalized settings, including domain adaptation, multilingual learning, and multi-task learning, with applications in information retrieval and information extraction. More recently, his work has focused on benchmarking large language models in multilingual and practical settings, including retrieval-augmented generation, medical QA reliability, and reasoning under increasing complexity.

## 2. Slightly longer research summary

I work on machine learning and natural language processing methods for generalized and realistic settings. My earlier work focused on domain adaptation, multilingual transfer, and multi-task learning for information extraction problems. More recently, I have been involved in building and extending benchmarks for large language models, especially in multilingual settings and retrieval-augmented generation. My current goal is to design evaluation settings that better reflect practical challenges, including noisy evidence, multilingual knowledge integration, and complex reasoning.
